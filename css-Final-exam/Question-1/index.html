Mount Fuji

   Mount Fuji is Japan's tallest peak and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its iconic conical shape and spiritual significance. It's a stratovolcano located on Honshu Island, straddling the border between Shizuoka and Yamanashi Prefectures. The mountain is a popular destination for tourists and climbers, especially during the climbing season from July to August.
